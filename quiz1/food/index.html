Local Food

Samarinda, the capital city of East Kalimantan, is renowned for its vibrant culinary scene, which beautifully reflects the rich cultural diversity of the region. One of the most iconic dishes is Soto Banjar, a traditional soup made with chicken or beef, flavored with a blend of aromatic spices and served with rice or vermicelli noodles. Another local favorite is Ikan Bakar, grilled fish marinated in a mix of spices, often accompanied by a spicy sambal, making it a must-try for seafood lovers. Additionally, Nasi Kuning, or yellow rice, is a popular dish served during celebrations, symbolizing prosperity and good fortune. The bustling street food stalls and local markets of Samarinda offer a delightful array of snacks and sweets, such as Kue Cubir, a traditional rice cake filled with sweetened coconut. Overall, the local cuisine of Samarinda provides a delicious glimpse into the region's heritage and flavors, making it a food lover's paradise.
